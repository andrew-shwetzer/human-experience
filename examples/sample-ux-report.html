Executive Summary

Skyvern's V2 landing page is a well-executed developer tool marketing page. The hero immediately communicates what Skyvern does ("AI agents to automate workflows on any website"), backs it with trust signals (HIPAA, SOC2, Y Combinator, 20.7K GitHub stars), and offers a clear primary CTA ("Start Building"). The page follows a strong information architecture: problem statement, use cases, build methods, developer integration, competitive comparison, security, and final CTA. The design is clean and professional with a warm cream palette and Plus Jakarta Sans typography. The major issue: the full-page screenshot reveals enormous blank sections where animations/lazy-loaded content failed to render, meaning ~60% of the page appears empty to crawlers and possibly to users on slow connections.

Step 5: Friction Inventory

Blank sections in full-page screenshot (~60% of page height)

HIGH

The desktop-full.png reveals massive empty white/cream areas between sections. The hero + client logos render, then blank space, then a small section, then more blank space. This suggests scroll-triggered animations, lazy-loaded content, or intersection observer patterns that don't fire during static full-page capture. This means: (1) search engine crawlers see an incomplete page, (2) users on slow connections may see blanks during load, (3) social media preview cards (og:image) would show empty content.

Evidence: desktop-full.png (page is 15,052px tall but rendered content fills maybe 5,000px)

Research: Google penalizes pages with significant above-the-fold content shifts. Lazy-loaded critical content may not be indexed.

Nav items and footer links fail tap target minimums

MEDIUM

Manifest shows many interactive elements at 18-19.5px height (well below 44px WCAG minimum). Nav items: Products (57x19.5), Use Cases (80x19.5), Pricing (43x19.5). Footer links: Features (58x18), Pricing (45x18). Discord icon in header is 32x32. These are fine for desktop mouse users but fail mobile touch standards.

Evidence: Manifest interactiveElements: tapTargetOk = false on ~15 elements

"Book a Demo" buttons are buttons, not links (no href)

MEDIUM

Multiple "Book a Demo" buttons are <button type="submit"> elements, not anchor tags. They presumably trigger JavaScript navigation or a modal. This means: (1) they won't work with JavaScript disabled, (2) users can't right-click to open in new tab, (3) screen readers may not announce a navigation destination. The footer "Book a Demo" IS a proper link to meetings.hubspot.com/skyvern/demo, creating an inconsistency.

Evidence: Manifest: interactiveElements index 9 (header "Book a Demo") is button type="submit" with no href. Index 46 (footer) is a proper link.

"Start Building" buttons also lack href

MEDIUM

Same issue as "Book a Demo." The primary conversion CTA is a button, not a link. Users who middle-click or Cmd+click to open in a new tab get nothing. This applies to "Get Started Free," "Start Building," "See Our Benchmark," "View All Integrations," and "Read the Documentation."

Evidence: Manifest: interactiveElements index 12, 13, 14, 25, 26, 29, 30, 31 are all button type="submit" with no href

Step 7: Priority Fix List

1

Fix blank sections: ensure content renders without scroll triggers

The full-page screenshots show ~60% blank space. This is likely intersection observer or scroll-triggered animations that don't fire for crawlers/static captures. Fix: ensure all content is in the DOM and visible by default, then ENHANCE with animations on scroll. Use CSS opacity animations triggered by intersection observer, but set the base opacity to 1 (not 0). This way crawlers, social previews, and slow-JS environments see full content.

DIMENSION: Responsive + Interactive EFFORT: Moderate IMPACT: SEO, social sharing, slow-connection users EVIDENCE: desktop-full.png
2

Convert button CTAs to proper anchor links

All primary CTAs ("Start Building," "Book a Demo," "Get Started Free," "See Our Benchmark," "View All Integrations," "Read the Documentation") are <button type="submit"> elements with no href. Convert to <a> tags styled as buttons. This enables: right-click open in new tab, screen reader destination announcement, works without JS, better SEO for internal linking.

DIMENSION: Interactive Quality, Trust EFFORT: Quick fix IMPACT: Accessibility, SEO, power user UX EVIDENCE: Manifest: 10+ buttons with no href
3

Add 1-2 customer testimonials with outcomes

Client logos and stats are strong, but zero customer quotes means the social proof is one-dimensional. Add 1-2 short testimonials with specific outcomes: "Skyvern replaced 40 hours/week of manual portal work for our accounts payable team." Place between the use cases section and the "Ready to automate" CTA for maximum conversion impact.

DIMENSION: Trust & Polish EFFORT: Quick fix (if quotes exist) IMPACT: Emotional arc peak + conversion lift
4

Increase tap target sizes for nav and footer links

Nav items are 19.5px tall, footer links are 18px tall. Add padding to meet 44px minimum for touch targets. The Discord icon in the header (32x32) also needs padding. This matters for tablet users who see the full nav but interact with touch.

DIMENSION: Responsive Quality EFFORT: Quick fix IMPACT: WCAG compliance, tablet usability
5

Add a product demo video/GIF that auto-plays on the hero

The dark product preview in the hero appears to be a static image or a video that didn't auto-play during capture. If it's a video, ensure it auto-plays (muted) to show the product in action. If it's static, consider replacing with a short GIF showing a workflow running. Seeing the product work is the strongest possible hook for a developer tool.
